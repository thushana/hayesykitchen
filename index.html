Bacon Bourbon Brownies

Pour in a little something extra special into the classic brownie.

5.10.2016 - San Francisco

I used to live a block away from where I do now, also on Hayes Street, in an apartment that shared a fire escape with one of the neighboring units. Our floor plans were mirror images of each other and our kitchens had a full size door and a full size window that opened out onto the fire escape.

My neighbors were lovely people. One in particular. Fascinating, more like it. A Sri Lankan born, Amherst, MA raised computer scientist who thought he knew a thing or two about design. His insatiable curiosity, big brown eyes, and a smile so sparkling white it glowed against his chocolate skin certainly caught my attention; not to mention his everyday-cowboy boots and shiny belt buckle style. I did my best to play it cool.

At this same time, I was also working part-time for a friend who had started his own food truck. I was able to make and sell desserts for the truck, which meant several recipe-testings and numerous batches to make. Which meant I needed taste-testers. Which meant I had a valid reason to constantly invite the brown cowboy over to give me an honest opinion. Come to find out, the amount of opinions he has to give is rivaled only by amount of questions he has to ask, and sparkling smiles he has to offer.

And, come to find out, that smile is filled with 28 sweet-teeth. Lucky for me, it wasn't that hard to convince him to taste a few recipes. Lucky for him, his neighbor had plenty of cups of sugar in the cupboard already.

It was only a couple weeks in when I learned of his love for chocolate...

Recipe & Ingredients

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Prepare a 9x13-inch baking pan by lining with parchment paper long enough to overhang the ends by a couple inches.

Cook in a large skillet, over medium heat:

  • 1/5 pound thick-slice bacon until well done, a.k.a. crispy.

Cool and drain cooked bacon on paper towels. DO NOT EAT. Chop instead and reserve the bacon fat for the brownie mixture.

Go ahead and cook the other half pound of bacon from that one pound package you most likely purchased. Feel free to devour this. Because if we're going to be health-minded enough to make bacon-bourbon brownies, we might as well be mindful of not letting that uncooked half to go to waste!

Preheat a double-boiler over low heat until water comes to a gentle simmer.

Chop:

  • 8 ounces bittersweet chocolate
  • 2 ounces unsweetened chocolate
  • 10 tablespoons unsalted butter

Combine the chocolates and butter together in the double-boiler, stirring continuously with a rubber spatula until completely melted and silky. Be sure to scrape the sides and bottom of the bowl while you stir to keep the chocolate moving to prevent burning. Lick spatula clean.

Whisk together in a medium sized mixing bowl:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 light brown sugar, packed
  • 1/4 cup bourbon
  • 3 tablespoons bacon fat (reserved from earlier)
  • 4 large eggs
  • along with the melted chocolate and butter

Whisk together in a separate mixing bowl until thoroughly combined and fluffed:

  • 1-1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Add the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, whisking thoroughly until combined with no lumps remaining and a gooey rich velvety batter is formed.

Pour batter into prepared baking pan and evenly sprinkle chopped bacon bits onto the the top. Place in oven and bake for 35-45 minutes, until the edges set and the middle remains slightly gooey when a toothpick is used to test for doneness.

Cool brownies in their pan on a wire rack for 10 minutes. Remove from pan by lifting out with parchment paper. Cut into 12 pieces, and serve... all to yourself.
